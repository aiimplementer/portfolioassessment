Portfolio Returns & XIRR ‚Äì FAQs
Simple explanations to help new investors understand portfolio returns clearly
1. Why do I see two different XIRR numbers in my report? ‚ñº
You see two XIRR numbers because they explain two different aspects of your portfolio. One focuses on how you invested your money over time, and the other focuses on how your money is performing today. Both are useful and serve different purposes.
2. What is Portfolio Health (Health XIRR)? ‚ñº
Portfolio Health shows how efficiently you invested your money over time. It gives more importance to periods when you invested larger amounts and reflects your discipline, timing, and consistency as an investor.

This XIRR helps you understand whether your investment habits are helping or hurting your long-term returns.
3. Why is Health XIRR important for new investors? ‚ñº
Health XIRR is especially useful for new investors because it focuses on building good investing habits. It highlights the impact of regular investing, patience, and avoiding poor timing decisions.

Improving discipline usually leads to better long-term performance.
4. What is Portfolio Performance (Performance XIRR)? ‚ñº
Portfolio Performance shows how your current money is performing today. It gives more importance to investments that have grown bigger in value and less importance to investments that have reduced in size.

This XIRR usually matches what investors feel when they look at their portfolio value on the dashboard.
5. Why can Performance XIRR be different from Health XIRR? ‚ñº
Performance XIRR is based on current portfolio value, while Health XIRR is based on how much money was invested. If some investments performed very well or very poorly, the two XIRR values can differ.

This difference is normal and expected.
6. Which XIRR should I look at first? ‚ñº
If you are reviewing your investing habits, start with Portfolio Health. If you want a quick view of how your money is doing today, look at Portfolio Performance.

Together, they give a complete picture of your portfolio.
7. Are both XIRR numbers correct? ‚ñº
Yes. Both XIRR values are calculated correctly using different weighting methods. They are not conflicting numbers; they simply answer different questions about your portfolio.
